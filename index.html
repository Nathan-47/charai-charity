What is TSW?

TWS is most commonly refers to “Tropical Steroid Withdrawal", a reaction that can happen when someone stops of reduces long-term use of tropical steroid creams or ointments, especially for eczema or other skin conditions. Tropical Steroid Withdrawal (TSW) is believed to be an iatrogenic (treatment-caused) condition, meaning it is caused by medication.

What happens in TSW

TSW is described as a cluster of symptoms that appear or worsen after stopping or cutting down tropical steroids, often after prolonged or high potency use.
Typical features include burning, very red or darkened skin, intense itching, oozing, flaking, swelling, red sleeves on arms/legs, temperature dysregulation, and sometimes nerve pain, fatigue, insomnia , and mood changes.

How funds will help

Funds will help provide medical support, specialist consultations, symptom management treatments, and essential daily care needs during recovery. It will also help support research and awareness to grant safer treatment practices and understanding for those affected by TSW.
